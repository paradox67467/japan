About Japan

Japan is an archipelago of over 6,800 islands stretching along the eastern coast of Asia — a nation where centuries-old temples stand in the shadow of gleaming skyscrapers, and ancient rituals coexist with cutting-edge technology.

From the serene bamboo forests of Arashiyama to the electric buzz of Shibuya crossing, Japan offers an experience unlike anywhere else on Earth. Every region tells its own story — through food, dialect, craft, and landscape.

The Japanese concept of mono no aware — the bittersweet awareness of impermanence — permeates everything: the fleeting beauty of cherry blossoms, the quiet grace of a tea ceremony, the mist over a mountain valley at dawn.

Culture

Experiences

🍵
Tea Ceremony
Participate in chado — the Way of Tea — a meditative ritual of preparing and drinking matcha that embodies harmony, respect, and tranquility.
⛩️
Shrine Visits
Walk through thousands of vermillion torii gates at Fushimi Inari, or meditate at ancient shrines tucked between moss-covered mountains.
🍜
Japanese Cuisine
From delicate sushi to steaming ramen to yakitori by the tracks — Japanese food is among the world's most sophisticated and diverse.
🏔️
Mt. Fuji
Climb Japan's most iconic peak at 3,776m, or admire it from the serene waters of Lake Kawaguchiko framed by cherry blossoms.
🎭
Traditional Arts
Witness kabuki theatre, ikebana flower arranging, or calligraphy — art forms refined over centuries and still thriving today.
♨️
Onsen
Soak in volcanic hot springs surrounded by snow or forest in ryokan inns that have perfected the art of Japanese hospitality.
🌸
Hanami
Join millions under blooming sakura trees each spring — a centuries-old tradition of celebrating the fleeting beauty of cherry blossoms.
🚄
Shinkansen
Ride the legendary bullet train across Japan at 320km/h — a marvel of engineering that connects the country seamlessly and punctually.

Did You Know

Fascinating Facts

  • 01

    World's Oldest Company

    Kongō Gumi, a Japanese construction firm founded in 578 AD, operated for over 1,400 years — the oldest verified company in history.

  • 02

    Vending Machine Nation

    Japan has approximately 5 million vending machines — one for every 23 people — selling everything from hot ramen to umbrellas to fresh eggs.

  • 03

    Most Michelin Stars

    Tokyo alone holds more Michelin-starred restaurants than any other city in the world, reflecting Japan's extraordinary culinary standards.

  • 04

    Forest Nation

    Despite being one of the world's most densely populated nations, over 68% of Japan's land is covered by forests and mountains.
